The Magnificent Meyersons

The story of one extraordinary New York family, on a day that starts out like any other, full of small hostilities, self-doubt, moments of tenderness, and the other quotidian battles and joys that comprise a normal day in the big city. But on this day, something unique will happen -- a scientific revelation, perhaps the most significant one in the history of humanity. This discovery, while more theoretical than actual, will affect each member of the family in a different way -- and, when, at this very moment of flux, a specter from the past comes back into their lives, they are forced to reevaluate their past decisions and their future choices.

THE MAGNIFICENT MEYERSONS revolves around one extraordinary New York family, on a day that starts out like any other, full of small hostilities, self-doubt, moments of tenderness, and the other quotidian battles and joys of a day in the big city. But on this day, something unique will happen -- a scientific revelation, an earth-shaking discovery. Each member of the family is affected in a different way -- and, when, at this very moment of flux, a specter from the past comes back into their lives, they are forced to reevaluate their past decisions and their future choices.

A shining light on both stage and screen, Kate Mulgrew is legendary for two very disparate roles: the bold but compassionate Capt. Kathryn Janeway on “Star Trek: Voyager” and the Russian prison chef Galina “Red” Reznikov on “Orange is the New Black”.

Richard Kind is known to many from his roles on “Mad About You”, “Spin City”, and “Curb Your Enthusiasm”, although children will be most familiar with him as the voice of the imaginary friend Bing Bong in Inside Out.

Barbara Barrie’s career has had many high points, from winning Best Actress at the Cannes Film Festival for One Potato, Two Potato, to receiving a Tony Award nomination for Company, to being nominated for both an Academy Award and Emmy Award for Breaking Away. She is also known to many for playing the wife of the namesake captain in “Barney Miller”.

Ian Kahn is best known as George Washington in AMC’s critically acclaimed series "Turn: Washington’s Spies". He also was a series regular on TNT's “Bull”, and has appeared in numerous shows both on and off-Broadway, as well as headlining at most major theater companies across the United States.

Jackie Burns holds the title of Broadway’s longest running Elphaba on Wicked. She also starred in If/Then, with her performance garnering rave reviews. Other original Broadway companies include the Tony Award-winning revival of Hair, as well as Rock Of Ages. She can be seen in the Netflix feature Set It Up.

Daniel Eric Gold is best known for his leading role in Amazon's critically acclaimed 1970's period drama, "Good Girls Revolt," based on the book by Lynn Povich. He also was one of the leads on ABC's iconic, award-winning series, 'Ugly Betty." In film, he can be seen films such as HACKER; CHARLIE WILSON’S WAR; DEFINITELY, MAYBE; TAKING WOODSTOCK; WAR OF THE WORLDS; BIRDS OF AMERICA; LAST NIGHT; and HARVEST. Other Television roles include "Chicago Fire," "The Blacklist," "Blue Bloods," and HBO's "Girls." Daniel is an OBIE award-winning actor with an impressive NY Theater career

Shoshannah Stern is perhaps the most successful young deaf actress in the world. She has had recurring roles on various TV shows: “Threat Matrix”, “Weeds”, “Jericho”, and “Supernatural”. She is also the first deaf person to create a network TV show, “This Close”, which she also co-stars in.

A presence across various media, Melissa Errico is perhaps best known for her stage work, in shows varying from “Les Miserables” to “My Fair Lady” to “Sunday in the Park with George”. She has been nominated for a Tony Award and a Lucille Lortel Award. Her most recent album, “Sondheim Sublime” displays her genius as an interpreter of the works of Stephen Sondheim.

Greg Keller has spent most of his career originating roles in new plays On and Off Broadway. Recently, he starred in the film The Neighbor’s Window, which won the 2020 Academy Award for Best Live Action Short. He has appeared on stage opposite Jane Fonda, Dianne Wiest, Rebecca Hall, Linda Lavin, Alicia Silverstone, Cynthia Nixon, Jeff Goldblum, and Alan Cumming, to name a few.

Lauren Ridloff stars as Makkari in the upcoming Marvel film, The Eternals, alongside Angelina Jolie and Salma Hayek. She is also a series regular on “The Walking Dead”, and was nominated for a Tony for Children of a Lesser God. Lauren is also a former Miss Deaf America.

Neal Huff is currently starring on Broadway in To Kill a Mockingbird as Bob Ewell, and is best known for his role as the Chief of Staff to the Mayor on The Wire. He has recently appeared in films Waves and Spotlight, and will be in the upcoming HBO limited series Mare of Easttown alongside Kate Winslet.

Lilli Stein is an actor and otherwise artist from New Hampshire. She is a member of Ensemble Studio Theatre where she spent most of her time performing before the first stage of the apocalypse dawned. Her television credits include The Marvelous Mrs. Maisel, BULL, The Blacklist, Instinct, and VEEP. Other film credits include All You Can Eat and The Privates. She is currently trying to learn how to play the mandolin as a very nice one was given to her on her birthday and the person who gave it to her is someone she sees often so she has to be sure to validate the quality of the gift.

T Slate Gray is a New York City based actor working in film, television and the theater. He has appeared in several films including A Little Game, Happy New Year. The Speed of Thought as well as regular appearances in television and commercials. T Slate wrote and starred in his award winning off Broadway one man show “Clipping In”. You might also recognize his hands from several commercial ads but don’t touch them!

Kate MacCluggage was raised in New England, but now makes New York her home. She’s made a career on and off-Broadway, on your favorite television programs, and in regional theaters around the county. Most recently, she played Mona in the world premiere of Samuel D. Hunter’s play Greater Clements at Lincoln Center, opposite Judith Ivey.

Ajay Naidu was most recently seen in The Kindergarten Teacher opposite Maggie Gyllenhaal and Gael Garcia Bernal, as well as his fan favorite role 'Shohid Akhtar' on NBC's Blindspot. Other film credits include cult classic Office Space, Richard Linklater’s SubUrbia (independent Spirit Award nomination), Bad Santa, Scary Movie 3, Hotel For Dogs, The Accidental Tourist, The Wrestler, The Honeymooners, The Guru, Hannibal, Requim For A Dream, Chutney Popcorn, and Pi. Television credits include “Social Distance”, "Billions", "God Friended Me", “Friends From College”, “Odd Mom Out”, “Deadbeat,” “Bored to Death,” “30 Rock,” “West Wing,” “Lateline” and “The Sopranos”.

Talia is a middle-schooler in New York City. Favorite roles she’s played on the stage include the Genie in “Aladdin”, the title character in “Annie”, and Captain von Trapp in “The Sound of Music”. Despite what her father says, she does not spend too much time on her electronic devices.

Evan Oppenheimer - Writer/Director

The Magnificent Meyersons is the seventh film written and directed by Evan Oppenheimer. His other films are Lost in Florence, A Little Game, The Speed of Thought, Alchemy, Justice, and The Auteur Theory. Evan’s prior films have all been released worldwide, and have won numerous awards. They’ve been New York Times Highlights, TV Guide Movie Picks of the Week, and Us Weekly Picks, while reviewers’ responses have ranged from “one of the best independent films of the year” to “a classic in its own right”. Evan is a graduate of Yale University and NYU Film School, and lives in New York with his wife and three daughters.

Edward Schmidt - Executive Producer

After graduating from the University of Michigan with degrees in mathematics and literature, Edward worked as an English teacher and Internet entrepreneur in Budapest, Hungary, and later became a technology and finance executive in London and New York.

He currently directs R&D for a large quantitative investment manager, and serves on the advisory board of the Museum of Mathematics in New York. Edward was a Producer of Lost in Florence and the Executive Producer (and Second Unit Director of Photography) on A Little Game.

Evan Wood - Editor

The Magnificent Meyersons is the second feature film edited by Evan B. Wood. The son of two teaching artists, Evan is a proud native New Yorker and a graduate of NYU Tisch School of the Arts, earning his BFA in Film and Television Production in 2016. In addition to his professional editing work, he is a documentary filmmaker and the creator of Just Another Runner, a docu-series about marathon runners with inspiring stories. He himself is an avid marathon runner and ambassador for people with Crohn’s disease.
